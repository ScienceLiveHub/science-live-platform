# For Organizations

## Structured evidence for better decisions

Organizations — companies, funding agencies, policy bodies — routinely need answers to research questions:

- "Is this finding reliable enough to build a product on?"
- "What does the systematic evidence say about this intervention?"
- "Has anyone independently replicated this result?"

Today, getting these answers means commissioning expensive literature reviews, trusting opaque AI summaries, or hiring consultants to read papers. The results are PDFs that can't be queried, updated, or verified.

**Science Live offers a different model: structured, queryable, verifiable evidence produced by credited researchers.**

---

## What you get

### Queryable evidence trails

Every nanopublication on the network is machine-readable. This means you can run structured queries across the entire knowledge base:

- "Which claims about [topic] have been independently replicated?"
- "What replication studies exist for [specific finding]?"
- "Show me all PRISMA systematic reviews on [intervention]"
- "What did researchers find when they tested [claim]?"

These aren't keyword searches through PDFs. They are semantic queries across structured, attributed, verifiable data — with full provenance back to the original researchers and papers.

### Verified attribution

Every nanopublication is cryptographically signed with the researcher's ORCID. You know exactly who produced each piece of evidence, when, and based on what sources. No anonymous AI summaries. No unattributed claims.

### Living evidence, not static reports

Traditional commissioned reports are snapshots — outdated the moment they're published. Nanopublication-based evidence is living:

- New replication studies add to the evidence base automatically
- Systematic reviews grow as new studies are screened
- You can subscribe to updates in your areas of interest

## Commission research

Science Live enables organizations to signal what research matters to them and fund the researchers who produce it.

### How bounties work

1. **Post a research interest.** Specify an area where you need evidence — a specific claim to replicate, a systematic review to conduct, or a domain to survey.

2. **Researchers respond.** Qualified researchers with expertise in your area produce structured nanopublications — replication studies, PRISMA reviews, evidence assessments.

3. **Fund the work.** Your funding goes directly to researchers as travel grants for conferences, research visits, and collaboration — not to the platform.

### Examples

| Your need | What researchers produce | What you get |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------|
| "Is this drug mechanism replicable?" | FORRT replication study + outcome nanopubs | Structured evidence with statistical results and methodology |
| "What's the state of evidence on X?" | PRISMA systematic review as nanopubs | Queryable evidence graph across multiple databases |
| "Which claims in this field are well-supported?" | AIDA claim nanopubs + replication outcomes | Machine-readable map of supported vs. contested claims |

### Why fund researchers directly?

- **You're not paying for a PDF.** You're funding the production of structured, permanent, reusable knowledge that benefits the entire research community.
- **Researchers get real recognition.** Travel grants, credits, and citable contributions — not invisible labor.
- **The evidence persists.** Unlike a consultant's report, nanopublications are permanent, queryable, and build on each other over time.

## The ecosystem advantage

Science Live is part of a distributed network. Your investment doesn't lock you into a single platform:

- Evidence is stored on the decentralized [nanopublication network](http://nanopub.net)
- Replication studies use [FORRT](https://forrt.org/) standards, publishable in the [R2 Journal](https://forrt.org/replication-hub/)
- Computational reproducibility links to [TIB Knowledge Loom](https://knowledgeloom.tib.eu) with machine-readable analysis proofs
- All data is open, queryable, and interoperable

**You're not buying a subscription to a platform. You're investing in a permanent, open knowledge infrastructure.**
