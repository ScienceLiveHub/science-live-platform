# Recognition & Credits

## Your work deserves recognition

The most valuable intellectual work in research — reading, evaluating, annotating, synthesizing, replicating — is also the most invisible. Science Live changes that.

Every nanopublication you create is:

- **Permanently citable** — with a persistent URI (e.g., `https://w3id.org/np/RA...`)
- **Signed with your ORCID** — cryptographically linked to your identity
- **Discoverable** — searchable by anyone on the nanopub network
- **Part of your scholarly record** — visible contributions that demonstrate your expertise

This isn't just metadata. When someone queries "what replication evidence exists for X?" and finds *your* nanopub, that's a direct, attributable contribution to science.

## The Science Live credit system

Science Live goes beyond citation by rewarding quality contributions with credits that have real-world value.

### How it works

```
Contribute → Earn credits → Build trust → Access funding
```

1. **Publish nanopublications.** Each quality contribution earns credits automatically. This includes literature annotations, citation assessments, replication study designs, replication outcomes, PRISMA review steps, and more.

2. **Get validated.** When peers engage with your work — citing it, building on it, or validating your findings — your trust level grows.

3. **Unlock advanced features.** The Explorer tier is **free forever**. Advanced features unlock through quality contributions (10 nanopublications + 5 validations), not payment.

4. **Qualify for travel grants.** Researchers who have built sufficient credits and trust in a given area can apply for travel grants funded by organizations that expressed interest in that research domain.

### Travel grants

This is the key mechanism that connects researchers to organizations:

- **Organizations** post interest in specific research areas (e.g., "climate adaptation", "drug mechanism replication", "DGGS benchmarking")
- **Researchers** who publish quality nanopublications in those areas earn credits
- **Qualified researchers** can request travel grants — funding for conferences, research visits, and collaboration trips
- **Organizations** that benefit from the research fund the grants

This creates a direct feedback loop: organizations signal what research matters to them, researchers produce it, and the funding flows to those who contribute most.

!!! info "Status"
    The credit system is being rolled out with the platform launch. Templates and infrastructure are ready. Details on credit thresholds and travel grant eligibility will be published as the first organizations come on board.

## Connected recognition initiatives

Science Live doesn't exist in isolation. It builds on and connects to established recognition frameworks in the open science community.

### FORRT and the R2 Journal

[FORRT](https://forrt.org/) (Framework for Open and Reproducible Research Training) operates the **[Replication Research (R2) Journal](https://forrt.org/replication-hub/)** — a diamond open access journal dedicated to replication studies. FORRT also runs the **[OR4 initiative](https://forrt.org/curated_resources/open-and-responsible-researcher-reward-a/)** (Open and Responsible Researcher Reward and Recognition), advocating for institutional recognition of open research.

Science Live provides the infrastructure layer for this vision:

- **FORRT templates** in Science Live let you publish structured replication studies
- **FORRT-KL templates** extend this with [Knowledge Loom](https://knowledgeloom.tib.eu) metadata for computational reproducibility
- Replication nanopubs published through Science Live are discoverable across the network and citable in the R2 Journal
- Contributors to the [FReD database](https://forrt.org/replication-hub/) (FORRT Replication Database) receive recognition on FORRT's contributorship page and potential co-authorship on manuscripts

### TIB Knowledge Loom

[TIB Knowledge Loom](https://knowledgeloom.tib.eu) is an Open Science digital library of analysis-ready, reproducible knowledge. It captures the full computational context of an analysis — methods, software packages, runtime environments, and results — as structured, machine-readable records (using [dtreg](https://arxiv.org/html/2512.10836), a data type registry for reproducible analyses).

Science Live's FORRT-KL templates link directly to Knowledge Loom records, creating a chain from claim → replication design → executable analysis → machine-readable proof.

### ORCID

All nanopublications are signed with your [ORCID](https://orcid.org), the global standard for researcher identification. This means:

- Your contributions follow you across institutions
- Name changes don't break attribution
- Your nanopub record integrates with your broader scholarly profile

## What counts as a contribution?

Any nanopublication you create and publish earns credits. Examples:

| Contribution | Template | Impact |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Annotate a paper with a citation relationship | CiTO | Builds the citation knowledge graph |
| Comment on or quote from a paper | Comment / Quote | Adds expert interpretation to the literature |
| Publish a formal research claim | AIDA | Creates a queryable, testable assertion |
| Design a replication study | FORRT / FORRT-KL | Structures the replication for reproducibility |
| Report a replication outcome | FORRT Outcome | Adds evidence for or against a claim |
| Define a PRISMA search strategy | PRISMA | Contributes to a systematic evidence base |
| Screen and assess studies | PRISMA | Advances a living systematic review |
